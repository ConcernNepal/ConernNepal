The Rise of Social Media in Nepali Politics

In Nepal,social media has become a pivotal force in shaping political landscapes and influencing electoral outcomes. Platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube have transformed political engagement, enabling real-time communication between candidates and voters, and facilitating rapid dissemination of political content.

The 2022 parliamentary elections in Nepal highlighted the dual-edged nature of social media's influence. While these platforms enhanced political participation, they also became conduits for misinformation. Doctored images and out-of-context videos were circulated to mislead voters, underscoring the challenges of digital misinformation in the electoral process.

Recognizing these challenges, the Election Commission of Nepal implemented a social media policy to address issues of misinformation, disinformation, and hate speech during elections. This initiative underscores the necessity for regulatory frameworks to ensure fair and transparent electoral processes in the digital age.

In this evolving digital landscape, political consulting has become increasingly vital. Political consultants offer strategic guidance, helping candidates craft cohesive messages and navigate the complexities of modern campaigns. Their expertise is crucial in leveraging social media effectively while mitigating risks associated with digital misinformation.

Globally, the role of political consultants has expanded globally exemplifying the impact of strategic campaign management. These consultants utilize data analytics and digital strategies to engage voters, reflecting a broader trend in political campaigning.
